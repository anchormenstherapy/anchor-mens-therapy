The reality

Fatherhood Is Hard. You Don't Have to Do It Alone.

Becoming a father — or struggling as one — can bring up feelings you didn't expect. The pressure to provide, protect, and "get it right" can be overwhelming. And when you're struggling, it's easy to feel like you're the only one.

Fatherhood changes everything — your identity, your relationships, your priorities. But most men don't get the support they need to navigate it. Society expects you to just figure it out, but that's not realistic.

Being a good father starts with being honest about what you need.

How it shows up

If any of this sounds familiar.

Fatherhood challenges show up in different ways. Some are obvious, some are quiet. All of them are worth addressing.

01

The pressure to provide is crushing.

You feel the weight of being responsible for another human being. The financial stress, the fear of failing — it never really turns off.

02

You feel disconnected from your child.

You love them, but bonding doesn't feel natural. You're going through the motions without feeling the connection you expected.

03

Your relationship has shifted.

Since becoming a parent, you and your partner feel more like co-managers than partners. Intimacy, communication, and fun have taken a back seat.

04

You're questioning if you're doing it right.

Every decision feels loaded. You compare yourself to other dads, to your own father, to some ideal that doesn't exist.

05

You've lost yourself in the role.

Your hobbies, your friendships, your identity outside of "dad" — it's all faded. You're not sure who you are anymore beyond this role.

06

The anxiety is constant.

About their safety, their future, your ability to be enough. It sits in the background of everything and makes it hard to be present.

How we help

A space to be honest about what fatherhood actually feels like.
