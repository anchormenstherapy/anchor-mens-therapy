Our story

Why I Started Anchor Men's Therapy.

About Jordan J. Caron →

My experience with gambling began in my early 20's with a single sports bet. What seemed harmless at the time slowly evolved into a long, off-and-on struggle that lasted more than a decade.

Like many forms of addiction, it didn't happen all at once. It built gradually, with periods of abstinence followed by relapse, financial consequences, and the constant mental burden that gambling creates.

For years, I minimised the problem. I told myself it would eventually stop. It wasn't until I sought gambling addiction counselling and began working with a gambling therapist that real, lasting change became possible.

Today, gambling has become more accessible and more aggressively marketed than ever. With the rise of legalised online betting platforms, constant advertising, and betting integrated directly into sports broadcasts, many men find themselves caught in patterns they never intended to develop.

Gambling addiction is not simply about money or willpower. It is often tied to stress, coping mechanisms, emotional regulation, and deeper psychological struggles.

At Anchor Men's Therapy, we understand these patterns.

Our goal is to provide grounded, confidential support for men who feel stuck, overwhelmed, or concerned about their relationship with gambling.

Because regaining control is possible.
