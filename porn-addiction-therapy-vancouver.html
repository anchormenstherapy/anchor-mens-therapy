Our story

Why I Started Anchor Men's Therapy.

About Jordan J. Caron →

Like many men, my exposure to pornography began early. What started as curiosity gradually became a pattern that followed me for years. At the time, I didn't recognise it as a problem.

Pornography had become so normalised, especially with the rise of the internet, that it was easy to dismiss the growing impact it was having on my confidence, relationships, and overall well-being.

Over time, I began noticing patterns that felt increasingly difficult to control. Habits that once seemed harmless were affecting important areas of my life in ways I didn't fully understand.

Seeking support changed that. Through therapy and personal work, I gained a deeper understanding of how compulsive behaviours often function, not simply as habits, but as coping mechanisms tied to stress, emotional regulation, and underlying psychological patterns.

This experience became one of the reasons Anchor Men's Therapy was created. Today, many men struggle quietly with similar challenges, often accompanied by frustration, shame, or confusion.

At Anchor Men's Therapy, we provide grounded, confidential support for men navigating compulsive pornography use and related concerns.

Because change is possible, and no one has to navigate it alone.
