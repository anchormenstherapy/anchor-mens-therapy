Why this matters

Meet Your Facilitator.

Like many men and perhaps yourself, my exposure to pornography began early. What started as curiosity gradually became a pattern that followed me for years. At the time, I didn't recognize it as a problem.

Pornography had become so normalized, especially with the rise of the internet, that it was easy to dismiss the growing impact it was having on my confidence, relationships, and overall well-being.

Over time, I began noticing patterns that felt increasingly difficult to control. Habits that once seemed harmless were affecting important areas of my life in ways I didn't fully understand.

Seeking support changed that. Through a men's group, therapy and personal work, I gained a deeper understanding of how compulsive behaviours often function — not simply as habits, but as coping mechanisms tied to stress, emotional regulation, and underlying psychological patterns.

For three years I facilitated a porn recovery group on another platform. Seeing how many men came through the group became the reason why I wanted to start this online porn recovery group. Too many men like yourself struggle quietly with similar challenges, often accompanied by frustration, shame, or confusion.

This porn recovery group provides a grounded, confidential support group for men navigating compulsive pornography use and related concerns.

Because change is possible, and no one has to navigate it alone.

Jordan J. Caron
Founder, Anchor Men's Therapy
